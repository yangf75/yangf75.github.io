  • Apr 11: Xingjian Hu (Fudan University)

    Title: The scaling limit of the volume of loop O(n) quadrangulations

    Abstract: In this talk we will discuss the O(n) loop model on quadrangulations. We will first introduce the model, its basic properties and several related work for preliminaries. Then we discuss the volume of maps and state our scaling limit result for the volume of the O(n) quadrangulations. In the end, we will state the outline of our proof and some observations of the relationship with branching random walk. This talk is based on joint work with Élie Aïdékon (Fudan University) and William Da Silva (University of Vienna).

  • Apr 18: Yang Xiao & Dongjian Qian (Fudan University)

    Title: Speed of Random Walks in Dirichlet Environment on a Galton-Watson Tree

    Abstract: This paper mainly deals with a transient random walk in Dirichlet Environment, or equivalently a linearly edge reinforced random walk, on a Galton-Watson tree. We compute stationary distributions of the environment seen from the particle in both quenched and annealed cases. With these stationary measures, we provide the almost necessary and sufficient criteria for positive speed and give a formula for speed when it is positive.

  • Jun 6: Xinyi Li (Peking University)

    Title:Non-existence of critical random fractals derived from Brownian motion

    Abstract: In this talk, we discuss various ``critical’’ random fractals whose Hausdorff dimension is 0: the pioneer triple points of 2D Brownian motion, the pioneer double cut points of 2D and 3D Brownian motion, and the double points on the boundary of clusters of 2D Brownian loop soup with critical intensity. We develop a unified approach to establish the non-existence of all these fractals above, which answers open questions from Burdzy and Werner in 1996 and Qian and Werner in 2019. Joint work in progress with Yifan Gao (CityU HK), Runsheng Liu (PKU) and Wei Qian (CityU HK).

  • Mar 7: Dominik Schmid (University of Bonn)

    Title: Biased random walk on dynamical percolation

    Abstract: We consider a biased random walk on dynamical percolation and discuss the existence and the properties of the linear speed as a function of the bias. In particular, we establish a simple criterion to decide whether the speed is increasing or decreasing for large bias. This talk is based on joint work with Sebastian Andres, Nina Gantert, and Perla Sousi.
